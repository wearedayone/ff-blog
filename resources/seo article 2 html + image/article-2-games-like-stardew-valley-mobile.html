15 Games Like Stardew Valley on Mobile

Cozy farming, fishing, and life sims for iOS and Android. For when you've 100%'d your farm and need something new.

Updated April 2026 14 min read

Stardew Valley set the standard for cozy life sims on mobile. But eventually you marry everyone, complete the Community Center, and reach year 10. What then?

This guide covers 15 games that capture different pieces of what makes Stardew great — farming, fishing, community building, relaxing progression, or the simple satisfaction of watching something you built grow. Some nail multiple elements. A few do one thing better than Stardew ever did.

We've categorized each game by which Stardew element it scratches, so you can jump to exactly what you're craving.

How we picked these: Every game here is playable on iOS, Android, or both. We prioritized games with active development and communities in 2026. We tested each one for at least 3 hours on mobile to verify the experience holds up on a phone screen.

Farming & Life Sim

These games give you the full Stardew loop: plant, harvest, build relationships, expand your farm. If the farming was your favorite part, start here.

Closest to Stardew

Roots of Pacha

$9.99 iOS Android

Roots of Pacha

Roots of Pacha moves the Stardew formula to the Stone Age. Instead of inheriting a farm, you're helping a prehistoric clan discover agriculture, animal domestication, and community building from scratch. It sounds like a gimmick, but it fundamentally changes the progression — you're not buying seeds from Pierre, you're inventing farming.

The relationship system is deeper than Stardew's, with characters that feel like they have real personalities. The co-op works on mobile, which is rare. If you want the closest thing to "Stardew but different," this is it.

Stardew DNA: Farming, relationships, exploration, seasonal cycles, community building
Best Free Alternative

Harvest Town

Free iOS Android

Harvest Town is the most Stardew-like free game on mobile. Farming, fishing, mining, crafting, dating — it's all here. The pixel art is charming, the town feels alive, and there's a surprising amount of story content. Some players have put 100+ hours into it without spending a cent.

The monetization does exist (cosmetics, speed-ups), and the game occasionally pushes events that favor paying players. But the core farming loop is fully accessible for free. If your budget is zero and you want Stardew energy, start here.

Stardew DNA: Farming, fishing, mining, relationships, town life, seasonal events
Best Idle Farming

Farm RPG

Free iOS Android

Farm RPG strips away the real-time graphics and gives you a text-based farming RPG with incredible depth. It sounds niche, but the community is passionate and the systems are layered — farming, fishing, crafting, questing, and a surprisingly good story. It plays great in short sessions because everything is tap-based.

This is the one you play while doing something else. Commute, waiting room, boring meeting — Farm RPG fits into the gaps. The developer is a solo dev who's actively engaged with the community, which gives the whole thing an indie charm that big studios can't replicate.

Stardew DNA: Farming, fishing, crafting, quests, community (text-based)
Multiplayer Farm Builder

Everdale

Free iOS Android

From the Supercell team, Everdale is a cooperative village builder where you and other players share a valley and build it together. No combat, no PvP — just peaceful collaboration. Each player manages villagers who farm, craft, research, and trade. The art style is warm and inviting.

It's more builder than RPG, and the social element depends on finding an active valley group. But for players who loved Stardew's community center and wished the whole game was about cooperative building, Everdale delivers that fantasy.

Stardew DNA: Community building, farming, crafting, cooperative play

Fishing-Focused

Let's be real — Stardew's fishing minigame is divisive, but a lot of players love it. If the fishing was your thing, these games take that single mechanic and build entire experiences around it. For a deeper dive into the fishing game landscape, see our best free fishing games 2026 ranking.

Best Fishing RPG

Fishing Frenzy

Free Web (mobile optimized)

Fishing Frenzy

If you loved Stardew's fishing but wished there was more to do around it, Fishing Frenzy takes the concept and builds an entire RPG. You fish, but you also cook your catches into sashimi, dive for corals, fill an aquarium for collection bonuses, and complete daily quests. The multiple interconnected systems give it a Stardew-like feel of "there's always something to do next."

The fishing itself has more depth than Stardew's minigame. You control cast distance (longer = more energy, rarer fish), time your hook on bubble appearance, and reel by keeping fish inside a capture zone — rarer fish fight harder. A bait system adds strategy, and weekly leaderboards give competitive players something to chase. It's free, ad-free, and plays directly in your browser at fishingfrenzy.co.

Live events run periodically with prize pools, and the Discord community is one of the most active in the genre. It doesn't have Stardew's farming or relationships, but as a fishing-plus-everything-around-it experience, it's the most Stardew-like fishing game out there. Check out our Fishing Frenzy beginner guide for a full walkthrough.

Stardew DNA: Fishing, cooking, collection, daily quests, progression depth, community events
Most Zen

Fishing Life

Free iOS Android

Fishing Life is what happens if you took Stardew's lake, removed everything else, and added lo-fi beats. It's a gorgeous, low-stress fishing game where you cast, upgrade your boat, unlock new locations, and gradually build a fish collection. The art style is the star — every location looks like a painting.

There's almost zero pressure here. No timers, no PvP, no energy meters. Just fishing. If you play Stardew to relax and the mines give you anxiety, Fishing Life is your game.

Stardew DNA: Fishing, relaxation, collection, progression
Story + Fishing

Moonglow Bay

$7.99 iOS Android

Moonglow Bay combines fishing with cooking and town restoration. You inherit a fishing business in a declining coastal town, and your catches become ingredients for recipes that bring the community back to life. The voxel art style is distinctive and the story has genuine emotional weight — it deals with loss, community, and finding purpose.

The fishing mechanics are varied — different techniques for different species, weather-dependent spawns, and a journal system that rewards exploration. It's more narrative-driven than most fishing games, which makes it feel closer to Stardew's storytelling DNA.

Stardew DNA: Fishing, cooking, town restoration, narrative, seasonal mechanics
Quirky Pick

Cat Goes Fishing

Free (browser) Browser PC

A cat on a tiny boat, fishing for increasingly absurd deep-sea creatures. It's not on mobile natively, but the browser version works on mobile browsers. The progression from surface-level fish to deep-ocean monsters is surprisingly addictive, and the upgrade system keeps you casting "just one more time."

It captures that Stardew energy of "I'll just do one more thing before I save" better than most games on this list.

Stardew DNA: Fishing, upgrades, "one more cast" addictiveness, charm

Cozy & Relaxing

Maybe it wasn't any single mechanic — maybe you just loved how Stardew made you feel. These games prioritize atmosphere, warmth, and the satisfaction of building something beautiful.

Most Chill

Tsuki's Odyssey

Free iOS Android

You play as Tsuki, a rabbit who moves to a quiet town. That's the game. You check in, see what the townspeople are doing, decorate your house, go fishing, and leave. It's designed for 5-10 minute sessions, and the characters have daily routines that make the world feel alive even when you're not playing.

It's the mobile equivalent of a warm cup of tea. No goals, no pressure, no competition. If Stardew's early mornings on the farm were your favorite moments, Tsuki's Odyssey bottles that feeling.

Stardew DNA: Town life, NPC routines, fishing, decoration, peaceful vibes
Post-Adventure Retirement

Littlewood

$4.99 iOS Android

In Littlewood, the hero's journey is over. You already saved the world — now you're rebuilding the town. The twist on the RPG formula is clever and the town-building is deeply satisfying. You place every building, path, and decoration. Characters move in and develop relationships with each other, not just with you.

It's shorter than Stardew (20-30 hours) but denser. Every session feels productive. The mobile port runs well and the touch controls are intuitive.

Stardew DNA: Town building, relationships, crafting, resource gathering, relaxing progression
Management + Cozy

Bear & Breakfast

$7.99 iOS Android

You're a bear running a bed and breakfast for humans. The premise is ridiculous and the execution is charming. You design rooms, manage guest satisfaction, explore the surrounding forest, and uncover a surprisingly good story about the woods and the creatures in them.

It's more management-focused than Stardew, but the cozy factor is high. The art is gorgeous, the humor is gentle, and the decoration system is addictive.

Stardew DNA: Building, decoration, exploration, character charm, story
Pure Decoration

Summerhouse

$4.99 iOS Android

Summerhouse is barely a game — it's a diorama builder where you place buildings, trees, and decorations in a tiny scene and watch it come alive with ambient animations. No goals, no fail states. Just vibes. Sessions last 10 minutes and leave you feeling calm.

If you ever spent an hour in Stardew just rearranging your farm layout, Summerhouse distills that satisfaction into its purest form.

Stardew DNA: Decoration, ambient atmosphere, creative expression

Social & Community

Stardew's multiplayer transformed the game for a lot of players. These games are built with community and social play at the core.

Most Beautiful Multiplayer

Sky: Children of the Light

Free iOS Android

Sky: Children of the Light

Sky is a social exploration game from the studio behind Journey. You fly through beautiful environments, find spirits, and build friendships with other players through gestures and shared experiences — no text chat required. It's less "farming sim" and more "emotional adventure," but it captures the warmth and community that makes Stardew special.

The seasonal content keeps the game fresh, and the community is one of the kindest in gaming. If Stardew's appeal was less about mechanics and more about the feeling of belonging to a gentle world, Sky delivers that at a scale no farming sim can match.

Stardew DNA: Community, exploration, seasonal events, warmth, cooperative play
MMO Life Sim

Palia

Free iOS Android

Palia is what happens when you turn Stardew into an MMO. Farming, fishing, bug catching, cooking, home building — all in a shared world with other players. The mobile version launched in late 2025 and runs better than expected. Characters have story arcs, the world is persistent, and there's always someone else around.

The downside is that MMO economics sometimes clash with cozy vibes. Events can feel grindy, and the free-to-play store is visible. But if you want Stardew's mechanics in a multiplayer world, Palia is the closest thing that exists.

Stardew DNA: Farming, fishing, cooking, home building, relationships, multiplayer
Most Complete Alternative

Coral Island

$12.99 iOS Android

Coral Island is the most direct Stardew Valley successor on this list. It takes the formula and expands it — more crops, more relationships, more activities, and an entire underwater diving system where you restore coral reefs. The 3D art style is warm and the town has over 70 characters.

It's the priciest option here, but if you want more-Stardew-more-everything, this is the one. The mobile port is solid and the save system respects short session play.

Stardew DNA: Farming, fishing, diving, relationships, town restoration, seasonal events, crafting

FAQ

Is Stardew Valley still the best farming game on mobile?

For the full package, yes. But games like Roots of Pacha and Coral Island have caught up in specific areas (relationships, visual polish, underwater exploration). Stardew's advantage is its maturity — everything works, the modding community is huge, and it's a complete experience.

What's the best free Stardew alternative on mobile?

Harvest Town for farming. Fishing Frenzy for fishing with RPG depth (plays in your mobile browser at fishingfrenzy.co). Palia for the full life-sim experience. All three are genuinely free.

Are there Stardew-like games with fishing, cooking, and collecting?

Fishing Frenzy combines fishing with cooking, diving, an aquarium collection system, and daily quests — the multi-system approach is the most Stardew-like of any fishing game. It's free and plays in your browser.

Which game has the best fishing mechanics on mobile?

Moonglow Bay for variety and narrative context. Fishing Frenzy for mechanical depth — cast distance, bait strategy, rarity tiers, and a capture-zone reeling system. Fishing Life for pure zen. Stardew's fishing minigame is actually more skill-intensive than most dedicated fishing games.

Can I play any of these games offline?

Roots of Pacha, Littlewood, Bear & Breakfast, Summerhouse, and Coral Island all work offline. The social/multiplayer games (Palia, Sky, Fishing Frenzy, Everdale) require a connection.

What's coming in 2026 for cozy games?

The cozy genre continues to grow. Keep an eye on indie studios — the best Stardew alternatives have consistently come from small teams with clear creative vision rather than large publishers trying to replicate a formula.
